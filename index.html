History of restaurant


Until the XVIII century. restaurant business everywhere was in a primitive state, limited to the service of roadside and city restaurants, taverns and similar establishments not the most aristocratic property. They rarely dared to visit married couples and even less often - women. Aristocratic circles of the population treated home cooks and dinners at secular receptions. Such conditions limited the development of the culinary business and the formation of service standards of public eating. Mentioned only one restaurant type - Tour d 'Argent, opened in 1533 in France and claimed a high level of service. The era of stagecoach significantly increased the mobility of the population, and the growth of urban population in the XVIII-XIX centuries. demanded for establishments and catering services. The revolution in the field of restaurant service was carried out by France, which has already developed a tradition of a special attitude to the art of cooking. Michele Boulanger, owner of a tavern in Paris, should be called the "father of the restaurant". The situation in this area of business was compounded by the dominance of the butchers' guild, which restricted the ability to cook and sell meat dishes while maintaining a medieval type of guild monopoly.
